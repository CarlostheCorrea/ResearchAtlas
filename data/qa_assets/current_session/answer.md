# Methods Summary of 'Experimental Search for Quantum Gravity'

The paper 'Experimental Search for Quantum Gravity' by Sabine Hossenfelder outlines various experimental methods to test quantum gravity models. The methods discussed include collider searches, particularly at the Tevatron and LHC, which explore the structure of matter at small scales and test the lowered Planck scale scenarios like the ADD and RS models. These experiments focus on detecting gravitons, modifications in cross-sections, and potential black hole production. Additionally, astrophysical processes provide complementary constraints, such as supernova cooling and cosmic gamma-ray background, which help bound the parameters of these models. The paper emphasizes the importance of combining different experimental areas to contrast predictions from various models.
