ResearchAtlas slide 1

Philosophical Challenges

  • Determining the right perspective for AI researchers
  • Addressing social impact issues effectively
  • Balancing ethical considerations

Speaker Notes

This slide discusses the philosophical challenges faced by AI researchers in the realm of social impact. The key issue is determining the appropriate perspective to take when addressing these complex issues, ensuring that ethical considerations are balanced with technological advancements.

ResearchAtlas slide 2

Technical Limitations

  • Focus on AI methodology problems
  • Misalignment with real-world needs
  • Need for practical, applicable solutions

Speaker Notes

Here, we explore the technical limitations of AI research for social impact. Often, the focus is on solving AI methodology problems, which may not always align with the practical needs of real-world applications. This highlights the necessity for solutions that are not only innovative but also applicable and effective in real-world scenarios.

ResearchAtlas slide 3

Interdisciplinary and Evaluation Challenges

  • Building partnerships with other fields
  • Time-consuming and complex collaborations
  • Need for new evaluation approaches

Speaker Notes

The interdisciplinary nature of AI for social impact requires collaboration with experts from various fields, which can be both time-consuming and complex. Additionally, traditional evaluation methods may not suffice, necessitating new approaches that emphasize partnerships, modeling, and evaluation to achieve tangible societal impacts.
