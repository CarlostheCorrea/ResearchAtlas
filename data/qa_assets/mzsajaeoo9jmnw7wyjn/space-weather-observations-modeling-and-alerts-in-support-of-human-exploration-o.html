Introduction to Space Weather Modeling

  • Utilizes 3D BATS-R-US Mars MS-MHD code
  • Simulates Mars' response to space weather
  • Focus on extreme events like Carrington-type

Speaker Notes

This slide introduces the primary tool used in the research, the 3D BATS-R-US Mars multi-species magnetohydrodynamic code. It is crucial for simulating how Mars reacts to significant space weather events, which are critical for planning future human exploration.

ResearchAtlas slide 2

Model Components and Processes

  • Includes Martian conducting core
  • Solves continuity equations for ion species
  • Uses momentum and energy equations for ion fluids

Speaker Notes

Here, we delve into the components of the model. It incorporates the Martian core and solves equations for different ion species, which are essential for understanding the dynamics of space weather interactions with Mars.

ResearchAtlas slide 3

Applications for Mars Exploration

  • Predicts space weather effects on Mars
  • Essential for human exploration planning
  • Supports technology deployment strategies

Speaker Notes

The final slide highlights the practical applications of the research. By predicting space weather effects, the model aids in planning human missions and deploying technology safely on Mars, ensuring mission success and safety.
