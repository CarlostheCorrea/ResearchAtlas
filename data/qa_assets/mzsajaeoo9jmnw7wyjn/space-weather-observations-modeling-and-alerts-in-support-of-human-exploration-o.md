# Space Weather Observations, Modeling, and Alerts in Support of Human Exploration of Mars

## Methods

The research utilizes the 3D BATS-R-US Mars multi-species magnetohydrodynamic (MS-MHD) code to model the response of Mars to extreme space weather events, such as a Carrington-type event. This model includes the Martian conducting core and solves separate continuity equations for each ion species, using one momentum and one energy equation for the ion fluids H+, O+, O2+, and CO2+. These simulations help predict the effects of space weather on Mars, which is crucial for human exploration and technology on the planet. The paper emphasizes the need for comprehensive space weather models to support future human exploration of Mars.

## Key Assumptions

- The Martian conducting core size
- Mantle resistivity
- Fully unmagnetized Mars

These assumptions were made to produce initial results that show significant surface magnetic fields during post onset of space weather events.

## Importance

The development of suitable models is expected to lead to a comprehensive operational Mars space weather alert (MSWA) system. This system would provide rapid dissemination of information to Earth controllers, astronauts in transit, and those in the exploration zone (EZ) on the surface by producing alerts that are delivered rapidly and are actionable.
