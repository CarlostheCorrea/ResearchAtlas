ResearchAtlas slide 1

Introduction to Mars Space Weather

  • Mars lacks a global magnetosphere and has a thin atmosphere.
  • Space weather understanding is crucial for human exploration.
  • Significant research is needed for Mars-specific models.

Speaker Notes

This slide introduces the unique challenges of space weather on Mars, emphasizing the absence of a protective magnetosphere and the thin atmosphere, which differ significantly from Earth. Understanding these factors is essential for future human exploration.

ResearchAtlas slide 2

Modeling and Alerts System

  • Development of Mars Space Weather Alert (MSWA) system.
  • Rapid information dissemination to Earth and Mars.
  • Use of magnetohydrodynamic models for extreme events.

Speaker Notes

Here, we discuss the proposed Mars Space Weather Alert system, which aims to provide timely alerts to astronauts and Earth controllers. The use of advanced modeling techniques, like magnetohydrodynamic models, is crucial for predicting extreme space weather events.

ResearchAtlas slide 3

Challenges and Future Directions

  • Impact of space weather on electrical systems and human safety.
  • Need for international collaboration and coordination.
  • Inclusion in the next Heliophysics Decadal Survey.

Speaker Notes

This slide highlights the potential risks of space weather on Mars, such as damage to electrical systems and radiation exposure. It stresses the importance of international collaboration and the need for space weather forecasting to be a priority in future research agendas.
