# Using the past to constrain the future: how the palaeorecord can improve estimates of global warming

## Question

What are they key take aways from the research, make it a downloadable MD file.

## Answer

The key takeaways from the research include the use of palaeoclimate records to improve estimates of climate sensitivity, which is crucial for predicting global warming. The study highlights the limitations of using only instrumental climate observations and suggests that incorporating geological timescale data can reduce uncertainties in climate sensitivity predictions. The research also discusses the use of probability distribution functions to express the range of climate sensitivity values from models, emphasizing the importance of model choice and parameter variations in these estimates.
