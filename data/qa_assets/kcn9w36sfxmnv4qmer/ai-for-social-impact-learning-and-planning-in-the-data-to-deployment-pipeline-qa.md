# AI for Social Impact: Learning and Planning in the Data-to-Deployment Pipeline

## Response

The data-to-deployment pipeline described in the research involves several key stages aimed at addressing AI for social impact problems. The pipeline is depicted in Figure 1 of the paper and includes the following stages:

1. **Immersion**: This stage involves gathering available data about the problem and immersing in the domain to understand the agents involved, the information they use, and the actions they can take. This often requires direct interaction with the participants and visiting the site of interaction.

2. **Predictive Model**: Based on the immersion stage, a predictive model is developed to handle latent information and make predictions about high-risk vs. low-risk cases.

3. **Prescriptive Algorithm**: The output of the predictive model is used to optimize the objective using game-theoretic or multiagent systems reasoning, often involving solving an optimization problem.

4. **Field Tests & Deployment**: The solutions are field-tested and compared to existing approaches. This stage involves returning to the field to evaluate the proposed solution and potentially iterating through the design process.

The pipeline emphasizes an end-to-end perspective, from data collection to field deployment, ensuring that AI solutions are effectively implemented in real-world settings.
