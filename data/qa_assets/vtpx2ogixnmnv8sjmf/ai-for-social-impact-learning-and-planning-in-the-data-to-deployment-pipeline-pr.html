Introduction to AI for Social Impact

  • Defines AI for Social Impact as a subdiscipline.
  • Focuses on measurable societal impact.
  • Targets vulnerable groups and underserved areas.
  • Emphasizes interdisciplinary partnerships.

Speaker Notes

In this slide, we introduce AI for Social Impact, a field that aims to create tangible societal benefits. Unlike traditional AI, it focuses on vulnerable populations and areas that have not yet seen significant AI advancements. Collaboration across disciplines is crucial, and evaluation methods differ from conventional AI research.

ResearchAtlas slide 2

Data-to-Deployment Pipeline

  • Characterized by stages: immersion, modeling, algorithm development, field tests.
  • Emphasizes real-world deployment.
  • Focuses on decision-making perspectives.
  • Highlights importance of understanding the problem context.

Speaker Notes

This slide explains the data-to-deployment pipeline, a structured approach to implementing AI solutions. It involves understanding the problem deeply, developing predictive models, creating algorithms, and testing them in real-world scenarios. The goal is to ensure that AI solutions are practical and effective in real-world decision-making contexts.

ResearchAtlas slide 3

Case Studies and Lessons Learned

  • Projects in public safety, wildlife conservation, public health.
  • Showcases impact of AI interventions.
  • Highlights importance of fieldwork and addressing data scarcity.
  • Stresses decision-focused learning for better outcomes.

Speaker Notes

Here, we discuss various case studies that demonstrate the impact of AI in fields like public safety, wildlife conservation, and public health. These examples illustrate the practical benefits of AI interventions. Key lessons include the necessity of fieldwork, overcoming data limitations, and focusing on decision-making processes to enhance intervention success.
